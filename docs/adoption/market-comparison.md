# Market Comparison

How does IUDX compare to leading data-exchange platforms? The table below summarizes the differences across the dimensions that matter most to adopters.

| Feature | Leading Data Exchange Platforms | **IUDX** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Barriers to Entry** | High: needs a central agency and security server; challenging for low-tech players. | **Low:** simple onboarding, SDKs/gateways, no mandatory installation. |
| **Real-Time Data Support** | Primarily transactional; real-time handling planned. | **Native support** for time-series, streaming, and async data. |
| **Authorization** | Certificate-based, coarse-grained, static; hard to manage and revoke granular access. | **OAuth2**, dynamic, resource/user-level **ABAC** — fine-grained and easy to manage. |
| **Data Interoperability** | Data-agnostic but needs prior agreement on formats; legibility is the consumer's/provider's responsibility. | Supports **heterogeneous data**, schema templates, and data dictionaries for interoperability. |
| **Future-Readiness** | Suited to static, high-assurance, slow update cycles; ecosystem-wide buy-in needed for changes. | **Agile**, designed for emerging tech and evolving standards — future-ready architecture. |

## What this means in practice

- **Easier to adopt** — low-tech players can onboard without standing up a central agency and security server.
- **Built for live data** — IoT, streaming, and async workloads are first-class, not retrofitted.
- **Granular, manageable control** — attribute-based access control replaces brittle certificate schemes.
- **Interoperable by design** — schema templates and data dictionaries remove the need for bespoke format agreements.
- **Future-proof** — an agile architecture evolves with emerging technology and standards.

## What sets IUDX apart

- **An open standard** — IUDX's architecture and API specifications have been adopted by the Bureau of Indian Standards as **IS 18003**.
- **Federated by design** — multiple, independently operated resource servers (including third-party servers) coexist in a single exchange, avoiding central data bottlenecks.
- **Consent-driven and auditable** — data is shared only with explicit provider consent, and every access is logged.
- **Open source** — a freely available Digital Public Infrastructure, already deployed across 50+ Indian smart cities and in agriculture, e-governance, and geospatial domains.
